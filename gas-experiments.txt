The diamond pattern introduces a marginal increase. The ~5k gas overhead comes from:
1. The fallback() dispatch - a cold SLOAD into the selectorToFacetAndPosition mapping (~2100 gas)
2. The DELEGATECALL opcode itself (~700 gas)
3. The diamond storage slot for ERC20 (keccak256-based pointer) vs plain slot access

The test_GasComparison_BurnAmounts test also reveals that the first burn costs more (~8400 vs ~3900 gas for subsequent burns) on the diamond side - because the first call warms up the diamond storage slot, which is cold for the keccak256-derived position.
